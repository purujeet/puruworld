How to safely answer the door when home alone

Mar 27, 2025 3 min read

Being home alone can be a common occurrence for many people, whether they live alone or their family members are out for the day. One of the most common anxieties people face when alone is answering the door. Whether it's a package delivery, a neighbor stopping by, or a stranger knocking, knowing how to safely answer the door can give you peace of mind and help you feel more secure in your own home.

Here are some tips on how to safely answer the door when home alone:

1. Look through the peephole or window first.
Before opening the door, always take a moment to look through the peephole or window to see who is on the other side. If you don't recognize the person, remain cautious and on guard. Trust your instincts and only open the door if you feel comfortable doing so.

2. Keep the chain lock on.
If you do decide to open the door, keep the chain lock on while speaking to the person on the other side. This will provide an extra layer of security and prevent them from being able to force their way into your home.

3. Have a plan in place.
Before opening the door, have a plan in place for how you will handle the situation. If you feel uncomfortable or unsafe, have a script ready to politely decline whatever the person is offering and close the door. If you are expecting a delivery or service person, have your identification ready to show them through the window or peephole.

4. Do not open the door fully.
When answering the door, only open it a few inches to speak to the person on the other side. This will prevent them from being able to push their way into your home if they are a stranger or someone you do not feel comfortable with.

5. Trust your gut.
If you have a bad feeling about the person at the door or something just doesn't feel right, trust your instincts and do not open the door. It's better to be safe than sorry in these situations.

6. Have a phone nearby.
When answering the door, always have your phone nearby in case you need to call for help quickly. This can provide you with peace of mind knowing that you have a way to reach out for assistance if needed.

7. Install a security camera or doorbell camera.
Another way to increase your safety when answering the door is to install a security camera or doorbell camera. This will allow you to see who is at your door without having to physically open it. Some doorbell cameras also have two-way audio, allowing you to communicate with the person without having to open the door.

8. Have a safety code word.
Create a safety code word with friends or family that you can use in case of an emergency. If you feel unsafe or threatened when answering the door, use the code word to let the other person know that you need help without alerting the person at the door.

9. Practice safety drills.
Just like fire drills, it can be helpful to practice safety drills for answering the door while home alone. This can help you feel more prepared and confident in how to handle different scenarios that may arise.

10. Report suspicious behavior.
If you notice any suspicious behavior or individuals lurking around your home, report it to the authorities immediately. It's always better to be proactive and prevent a potentially dangerous situation from escalating.

In conclusion, answering the door when home alone can be a nerve-wracking experience, but with the right precautions and safety measures in place, you can feel more confident and secure in your own home. Trust your instincts, have a plan in place, and follow these tips to ensure that you remain safe when answering the door. Remember, it's always better to be cautious and take steps to protect yourself rather than risking your safety.
