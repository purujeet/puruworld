How to Make Your Own Kombucha at Home

Dec 26, 2025 4 min read

Kombucha has become increasingly popular in recent years for its health benefits and delicious taste. This fermented tea drink is not only tasty but also has a plethora of health benefits, including promoting gut health, improving digestion, and boosting the immune system. Plus, making your own kombucha at home is simple and cost-effective. In this blog, we will guide you through the process of making your own kombucha at home.

First, let's break down what kombucha actually is. Kombucha is a fermented tea drink that is made by fermenting sweetened tea with a culture of yeast and bacteria known as a "SCOBY" (symbiotic culture of bacteria and yeast). The fermentation process creates a slightly effervescent, tangy drink that is both refreshing and beneficial for your body.

To make your own kombucha at home, you will need the following ingredients and equipment:

- 1 SCOBY (you can purchase one online or ask a friend who brews their own kombucha for a spare)
- 1 cup of sugar
- 8 bags of black tea (or 2 tablespoons of loose tea)
- 14 cups of water
- 2 cups of starter tea (previously brewed kombucha)
- A large glass jar or container
- A clean cloth or paper towel
- A rubber band

Now that you have gathered all of your ingredients and equipment, it's time to start brewing your own kombucha. Here is a step-by-step guide to making your own delicious and healthy kombucha at home:

Step 1: Prepare the tea mixture
Start by boiling 4 cups of water in a large pot. Once the water is boiling, remove the pot from the heat and add the tea bags or loose tea. Let the tea steep for 10-15 minutes before removing the tea bags or straining out the loose tea. Stir in the sugar until it has completely dissolved.

Step 2: Add water and starter tea
Pour the remaining 10 cups of water into your glass jar or container. Add the tea mixture and the starter tea (previously brewed kombucha) to the jar and stir to combine.

Step 3: Add the SCOBY
Carefully place the SCOBY into the jar, making sure it is fully submerged in the tea mixture. Cover the jar with a clean cloth or paper towel and secure it with a rubber band. This will allow air to flow in and out of the jar while keeping out any contaminants.

Step 4: Ferment the kombucha
Place the jar in a warm, dark place (such as a cupboard or pantry) and let it ferment for 7-10 days. Check the taste of the kombucha every few days to see if it has reached your desired level of tanginess. The longer you ferment the kombucha, the more vinegary it will become.

Step 5: Bottle the kombucha
Once the kombucha has reached your desired level of fermentation, it's time to bottle it. Carefully remove the SCOBY from the jar and set it aside for your next batch of kombucha. Pour the kombucha into glass bottles, leaving about an inch of space at the top of each bottle. Seal the bottles with a tight-fitting lid.

Step 6: Second fermentation (optional)
If you want to add flavor to your kombucha, you can do a second fermentation by adding fruit, herbs, or spices to the sealed bottles. Let the kombucha sit at room temperature for an additional 3-5 days before refrigerating it.

Congratulations, you have successfully made your own kombucha at home! Enjoy your homemade brew and experiment with different flavors and ingredients to find your perfect recipe.

Tips for making your own kombucha at home:

- Make sure all of your equipment is clean and sanitized before you start brewing.
- Use organic ingredients whenever possible to ensure the highest quality kombucha.
- Experiment with different tea blends and flavorings to create unique and delicious kombucha recipes.
- Store your bottled kombucha in the refrigerator to slow down the fermentation process and keep it fresh.

In conclusion, making your own kombucha at home is a fun and rewarding process that allows you to enjoy this delicious and healthy drink whenever you want. By following the steps outlined in this blog, you can create your own batch of kombucha that is both tasty and good for your body. So, why not give it a try and start brewing your own kombucha at home today? Cheers to good health and happy brewing!
