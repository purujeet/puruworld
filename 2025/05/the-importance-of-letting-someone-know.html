The importance of letting someone know your route before going out

May 6, 2025 4 min read

It's a beautiful day outside, and you're feeling the urge to lace up your running shoes and hit the trails for a long run. Or maybe you're planning to embark on an epic hike through the mountains, or take a solo bike ride through the countryside. Whatever outdoor adventure you have in mind, it's always important to let someone know your route before you head out.

While it may seem like a hassle to have to share your plans with someone else, the reality is that doing so can potentially save your life in an emergency. Whether you're planning to go for a run in the city or a hike in the wilderness, letting someone know your route can ensure that help is on hand should anything go wrong.

One of the most compelling reasons to share your route is safety. If you were to get injured or lost while out on the trail, having someone who knows where you were planning to go can greatly expedite search and rescue efforts. Instead of having to search the entire area blind, rescuers can focus their efforts on the specific route you had planned to take. This can mean the difference between being found quickly and safely, or potentially spending days lost in the wilderness.

Additionally, if you were to become incapacitated or unable to call for help, having someone who knows your route can ensure that help is on the way as soon as possible. This is especially important in remote or sparsely populated areas where cell phone reception may be limited or non-existent. By sharing your route, you increase the likelihood that someone will notice if you fail to return at the expected time and will be able to alert authorities to begin a search.

Another important reason to share your route is accountability. When you let someone know where you plan to go, you are creating a sense of responsibility to stick to that plan. It's all too easy to deviate from your intended route, whether due to poor planning, lack of preparedness, or simply getting carried away in the moment. By knowing that someone else is aware of your plans, you are more likely to stay on track and make safe decisions while out in the wilderness.

Sharing your route also gives your loved ones peace of mind. When you head out on an outdoor adventure, your friends and family may understandably worry about your safety. By sharing your route with someone you trust, you can reassure them that you have a plan in place and know how to stay safe while out in the wild. This can help alleviate anxiety and ensure that your loved ones are confident in your ability to handle any challenges that may arise.

In addition to safety and peace of mind, sharing your route can also prove helpful in more mundane situations. For example, if you were to run out of water or supplies while on a long hike, having someone who knows your route could make it easier to coordinate a rescue or supply drop. Likewise, if you were to encounter a technical issue with your bike or vehicle, a friend who knows your plans could come to your aid more quickly and efficiently.

So how should you go about sharing your route with someone before heading out on an outdoor adventure? There are several ways to do so. You could simply text or call a friend or family member with your plans, including the route you plan to take, expected duration, and any backup plans in case of emergencies. You could also use a GPS tracking app to share your location in real-time with someone else, allowing them to follow your progress and be alerted if you go off course or encounter trouble.

No matter how you choose to share your route, the most important thing is to actually do it. Taking a few minutes to communicate your plans with someone else before heading out on an outdoor adventure can mean the difference between a safe and enjoyable experience, or a potentially dangerous situation. So next time you head out for a run, hike, or bike ride, remember to let someone know your route  your safety and peace of mind may depend on it.
