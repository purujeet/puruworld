How to protect yourself from being followed

Mar 4, 2026 3 min read

In today's world, it's become increasingly important to protect ourselves from being followed. Whether you're walking home alone at night or simply want to ensure your privacy isn't being invaded, being aware of your surroundings and taking certain precautions can help keep you safe. In this blog, we'll discuss some practical tips on how to protect yourself from being followed.

1. Be aware of your surroundings

The first step in protecting yourself from being followed is to be aware of your surroundings. Take note of who is around you and pay attention to any suspicious behavior. Trust your instincts - if something doesn't feel right, it probably isn't. Stay alert and try to avoid areas that are dimly lit or deserted, especially at night.

2. Change up your routine

If you suspect that you are being followed, try to vary your routine. Take different routes home, change up your schedule, and avoid going to the same places at the same times every day. This can help throw off anyone who may be trying to track your movements.

3. Let someone know where you are

If you're out alone and feel unsafe, let someone know where you are. Share your location with a trusted friend or family member and check in regularly. This way, someone will know if something is wrong and can help you if needed.

4. Trust your instincts

If you feel like someone is following you, don't ignore it. Trust your instincts and take action. Cross the street, go into a store, or walk towards a crowded area. If you're driving, make several turns to see if the car behind you follows. If you're on foot, don't hesitate to ask for help or call the police if you feel threatened.

5. Stay in well-lit areas

When walking alone at night, stick to well-lit areas and avoid shortcuts through dark alleys or isolated paths. Being in a public, well-lit space can deter potential attackers and make it easier for others to see if you're in trouble.

6. Use technology to your advantage

In today's digital age, there are many apps and devices that can help keep you safe. Consider downloading a personal safety app that allows you to quickly call for help or share your location with others. You can also carry a personal alarm or whistle that you can use to attract attention in case of an emergency.

7. Learn self-defense techniques

Taking a self-defense class can give you the skills and confidence to protect yourself in dangerous situations. Learn basic techniques to defend yourself and practice them regularly. Knowing how to fight back can make a big difference in a potentially dangerous situation.

8. Avoid sharing personal information

Be mindful of what you share online and in person. Avoid posting your exact location on social media and refrain from sharing personal details with strangers. Be cautious when giving out your phone number or address, and use privacy settings to limit who can see your information.

9. Be prepared to defend yourself

If you find yourself in a situation where you need to defend yourself, be prepared to take action. Use any objects around you as weapons, such as keys, pens, or even your bag. Make noise and attract attention to scare off your attacker. Remember, your safety is worth more than any belongings.

10. Seek help if needed

If you feel like you're being followed or are in danger, don't hesitate to seek help. Call the police, ask a stranger for assistance, or head to a nearby business or home for safety. Your well-being is important, and there's no shame in reaching out for help when you need it.

In conclusion, protecting yourself from being followed requires vigilance, awareness, and preparedness. By following these tips and being proactive about your safety, you can reduce the risk of being followed and stay safe in any situation. Remember to trust your instincts, stay alert, and take steps to protect yourself at all times. Stay safe and stay aware.
