How to Start Writing a Novel from Scratch

Feb 15, 2026 5 min read
Advertisement

So, you want to write a novel. You have a great idea bouncing around in your head, but you're not quite sure how to get started. Don't worry - every writer, no matter how experienced, has faced this daunting task at one time or another. But the good news is that there are plenty of strategies and tips to help you get started on your journey to becoming a novelist. In this blog post, we'll discuss how to start writing a novel from scratch, including brainstorming ideas, developing characters, outlining your plot, and more.

1. Find Your Inspiration

Before you can start writing a novel, you need to have a solid idea to build upon. Inspiration can come from anywhere - a dream, a conversation you overheard, a news story, or even a personal experience. Once you have a spark of inspiration, it's important to develop it further. Ask yourself questions like: What is the central conflict of the story? Who are the main characters? What are their motivations and goals? What is the setting of the story? By answering these questions, you can start to flesh out your idea and turn it into a fully-formed concept for your novel.

2. Develop Your Characters

Characters are the heart of any novel, so it's crucial to spend time developing them before you start writing. Start by creating detailed character profiles for your main characters, including their background, personality traits, motivations, and goals. Consider how they will change and grow throughout the course of the story, and how they will interact with each other. By creating fully-realized characters, you can ensure that your novel will be engaging and relatable to readers.

3. Outline Your Plot

Once you have your idea and characters in place, it's time to outline your plot. This is a roadmap of the major events that will happen in your novel, from beginning to end. Some writers prefer to create a detailed outline, while others prefer to have a more flexible plan. Whatever method you choose, make sure to include key plot points such as the inciting incident, the climax, and the resolution. This will help you stay focused and on track as you write your novel.

4. Set Aside Time to Write

One of the biggest challenges of writing a novel is finding the time to actually sit down and write. With busy schedules and endless distractions, it can be difficult to carve out time for writing. However, it's important to prioritize your writing time if you want to make progress on your novel. Set aside a specific time each day or week to dedicate to writing, whether it's early in the morning, late at night, or during your lunch break. By making writing a priority, you can ensure that you make steady progress on your novel.

5. Start Writing

Now that you have your idea, characters, plot outline, and dedicated writing time, it's time to start writing your novel. Remember that the first draft is just that - a draft. It doesn't have to be perfect, so don't get too caught up in editing or reworking every sentence. The most important thing is to get your ideas down on paper and keep moving forward. Allow yourself to make mistakes and experiment with different writing styles and techniques. Remember, you can always go back and revise later.

6. Stay Motivated

Writing a novel is a long and often challenging process, so it's important to stay motivated and keep yourself inspired. One way to stay motivated is to set goals for yourself, whether it's a word count goal for each day or week, or a deadline for completing certain chapters. Celebrate small victories along the way, such as finishing a difficult scene or reaching a milestone in your word count. Surround yourself with a support system of fellow writers or friends and family who can encourage you and provide feedback on your work. Most importantly, remember why you started writing in the first place, and let that passion drive you forward.

7. Revise and Edit

Once you have completed your first draft, it's time to revise and edit your novel. This is where the real work begins, as you go through your manuscript with a critical eye, looking for inconsistencies, plot holes, and areas that need improvement. Consider enlisting the help of beta readers or hiring a professional editor to provide feedback on your novel. Keep in mind that revision is an essential part of the writing process, and it may take several rounds of edits before your novel is ready for publication.

8. Seek Feedback

Finally, once you have revised and edited your novel, it's time to seek feedback from others. Share your work with beta readers, writing groups, or online writing communities to get fresh perspectives on your story. Remember that feedback is a valuable tool for improving your writing and making your novel the best it can be. Be open to constructive criticism and be willing to make changes based on the feedback you receive. By seeking feedback from others, you can gain valuable insights and make your novel stronger and more compelling.

In conclusion, starting to write a novel from scratch can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and strategies, you can turn your idea into a fully-realized manuscript. By finding inspiration, developing characters, outlining your plot, setting aside time to write, staying motivated, revising and editing, and seeking feedback, you can navigate the writing process with confidence and create a novel that resonates with readers. So start writing today and embark on your journey to becoming a novelist. Good luck!
